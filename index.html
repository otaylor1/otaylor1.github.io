About Me

Front-End Developer/Designer

Hello! If you have made it this far, I can only assume that you would like to know a little about my background.
I earned my B.A. in General Studies with a focus in Education (Magna Cum Laude: 3.595 GPA) from the University of Pittsburgh. After graduating, I completed a 3-month apprenticeship at We Can Code It in Cleveland, Ohio. There, I was introduced to C#, SQL, and HTML. Although the goal of the apprenticeship is to prepare one to become a junior full stack developer, I realized that I have a love for the front-end side of things. I enjoy designing as well as the challenge of taking a mockup, and solving the puzzle of duplicating it using code. Puzzles are fun! Well, at least I think so.

Take Care

Date: March 2018

This app is designed for a user who is the primary caretaker of an elderly adult such as a mother or father. This app allows for a single place to can keep track of appointments and medications.

  • User needs to login / create an account.
  • User needs to add appointments.
    • User needs to update appointment information.
  • User needs to add medication.
  • User needs to confirm that meds were taken at scheduled time.
  • User needs to update medication information.

Adobe Illustrator was used for the design of this project.

SafeKeepers QR

Date: August 2016

Safekeepers QR is an app idea that I was able to bring to life with the help of a team in my coding bootcamp. Safekeepers QR allows for easy posting of a child’s allergies, medical conditions, and other critical health data, that is connected to a QR code that can be printed and applied to clothing, backpacks, and other wearables. This allows emergency responders instant access to vital information. Once the QR code is scanned, emergency contacts are instantly notified the location of the child via text message, and emergency responders are able to focus on the child’s needs.
In this project, my main focus was on the UX/UI Design along with a portion of the development of the varying views. This project is not currently live, but the video above can give you an idea of the design and functionality.
